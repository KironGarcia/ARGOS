===== Session 2026-03-06 — First successful test of mic, speaker and LEDs =====

**Objective:** Validate microphone (INMP441), amplifier/speaker (PAM8403 + 4Ω 3W), and status LEDs (GPIO 5, 18) on the ESP32.

**Approach:** Started with the combined test sketch ''1test.ino'' (menu: mic, speaker, LEDs). Then switched to separate sketches to isolate issues.

==== Problems encountered and fixes ====

* **LEDs not responding / speaker hiss when driving LEDs:** When all subsystems shared the same power (VIN for amplifier + LEDs), the LEDs did not turn on and the speaker produced hiss when the LED signal was sent. Solution: test LEDs separately (''teste_só_luces.ino'') and keep LED current path independent from the amplifier supply.

* **LED polarity:** LEDs were initially soldered with the resistor and signal in the wrong arrangement. Correct wiring: **signal (GPIO)** → resistor → **long leg (anode)** of LED → **short leg (cathode)** → GND. The long leg is positive; the resistor must be in series on the path to GND, not between both legs in a way that bypasses GPIO control.

* **LEDs always on:** After fixing polarity, the LEDs stayed on constantly and the program had no effect. Cause: a **VCC (3V3 or VIN) wire was also connected to the positive (long) leg** of each LED alongside the GPIO signal. The GPIO cannot turn the LED off if VCC is always feeding the anode. **Fix:** Remove the VCC wire from the LED positive side. Only the GPIO pin (D5 for one LED, D18 for the other) must supply the positive side; no separate power wire. After cutting the VCC cable from each LED, the LEDs responded correctly to the sketch.

==== Outcome ====

* **Microphone:** Working. Tested with ''teste_só_microfone.ino'' (10 s capture, analysis). Result: "MICROFONE FUNCIONOU" with levels consistent with voice.
* **Speaker (amplifier + 4Ω 3W):** Working. Tested with 1test option 2 (sweep tone). Volume is strong; no hardware issues.
* **LEDs (yellow GPIO 5, red GPIO 18):** Working. Tested with ''teste_só_luces.ino'' (both LEDs fade together, 5 s cycle). Correct connection: GPIO → resistor → LED anode → LED cathode → GND; no VCC on the LED anode.

==== Decision: LEDs removed from project ====

After confirming the LEDs worked, we decided to **remove them from the Alexa system entirely**. Reasons: the LEDs did not provide the illumination we needed; they added complexity without real benefit; and they caused interference (hiss in the speaker when driven). To keep the setup simple and avoid crosstalk, the project now uses **ESP32 with microphone and speaker only** — no status LEDs in the final hardware.

**Current state:** Alexa China hardware = ESP32 + INMP441 microphone + PAM8403 + 4Ω 3W speaker. No LEDs. Ready to proceed with integrated firmware and next steps (e.g. wake word, UART).
