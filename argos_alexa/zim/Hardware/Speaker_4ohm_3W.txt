====== Speaker 4Ω 3W ======
Created Monday 10 February 2026

===== Overview =====

A 4Ω 3W speaker is used for audio output from the "Alexa China" voice system. It provides clear, audible voice responses for the ARGOS demonstration.

**Role in Project:** Audio output for TTS (Text-to-Speech) responses  
**Type:** Dynamic speaker (electromagnetic coil)  
**Connection:** PAM8403 amplifier output  
**Power:** 3W maximum (from PAM8403 amplifier)

===== Impedance Matching =====

==== Why 4Ω? ====

**PAM8403 Amplifier:**
* Designed for 4Ω or 8Ω speakers
* 4Ω = maximum power output
* 3W per channel @ 4Ω load

**Power Transfer:**
* **4Ω speaker:** Maximum power transfer ✅
* **8Ω speaker:** Less power (~1.5W), but works
* **2Ω speaker:** Too low, may damage amplifier ❌

**For ARGOS:**
* 4Ω 3W speaker is perfect match
* Optimal power transfer
* Maximum efficiency

===== Connection to PAM8403 =====

==== Wiring Diagram ====

**Speaker Connections:**
```
PAM8403 Amplifier    →    Speaker
├── OUT+            →    Speaker positive terminal
└── OUT-            →    Speaker negative terminal
```

**Polarity:**
* **Important:** Connect correctly (OUT+ to +, OUT- to -)
* **Reversed:** Works but phase inverted (usually not noticeable for voice)
* **Best practice:** Match polarity for consistency

**Wire Specifications:**
* **Gauge:** 20-24 AWG (sufficient for 3W)
* **Length:** Keep short (<30 cm) for best performance
* **Type:** Stranded wire (flexible)

===== Troubleshooting Guide =====

==== Common Issues ====

**Problem: No sound**
* Check: Speaker connections (OUT+ and OUT-)
* Check: PAM8403 power supply
* Check: PAM8403 input signal (ESP32 DAC)
* Check: Speaker impedance (should be 4Ω)
* Check: Volume level (DAC output)

**Problem: Distorted sound**
* Check: Power supply voltage (should be stable)
* Check: Input signal level (may be too high)
* Check: Speaker power rating (not exceeding 3W)
* Check: Impedance mismatch

**Problem: Low volume**
* Check: Power supply voltage (5V better than 3.3V)
* Check: Input signal level (DAC output)
* Check: Speaker impedance (4Ω correct)
* Check: Distance from speaker

**Problem: Buzzing/humming**
* Check: GND connection (common ground essential)
* Check: Power supply quality (clean voltage)
* Check: Cable routing (away from digital signals)
* Check: Electromagnetic interference

===== Integration Notes =====

==== System Integration ====

**Complete Audio Chain:**
```
ESP32 DAC (GPIO 25)
    ↓
PAM8403 Amplifier (IN+)
    ↓
PAM8403 Amplifier (OUT+)
    ↓
Speaker (4Ω 3W)
    ↓
Audio Output (Voice Responses)
```

**Signal Levels:**
* ESP32 DAC: 0-3.3V (8-bit, 256 levels)
* PAM8403 Input: 0-3.3V (amplified)
* PAM8403 Output: 0-5V (or 0-3.3V) PWM
* Speaker: 3.5V RMS @ 3W

**Power Flow:**
* ESP32: ~100 mA (DAC + processing)
* PAM8403: ~300-400 mA (amplification)
* Speaker: ~870 mA @ 3W (audio output)
* **Total:** ~400-500 mA (typical voice output)
