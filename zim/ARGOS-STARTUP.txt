====== ARGOS-STARTUP ======
General Description and Initial Canvas of the ARGOS Project
Created Tuesday 04 March 2026

===== 1. Project Overview =====

ARGOS is an IoT security and smart-home protection startup. It originated as IOTRAC and evolved into ARGOS with a focus on **smart homes** and the **middle-class** segment. The product combines a mobile application (monitoring, protection, education), an educational chatbot (ArgosBot), and physical proof-of-concept devices (autonomous car and vulnerable "Alexa China" device) to demonstrate real-world attacks and ARGOS's response.

**Ecosystem:**
* **ARGOS Application** — IoT security monitoring, device management, and educational platform (backend + frontend + chatbot).
* **ARGOS Car** — Mini autonomous vehicle used in live demos to show protection in action (Raspberry Pi, motors, sensors).
* **ARGOS Alexa (China)** — ESP32-based vulnerable voice device; attack vector for the security demo (Stage 2).

---

===== 2. Value Proposition =====

ARGOS is not only an attack blocker; it is an **educator in security**.

* **Core offer:** Domestic IoT protection for the middle class — gamified, educational, and accessible.
* **Dual problem:** (1) End users do not realise they are at risk; (2) Large organisations (e.g. banks) suffer attacks that originate from domestic botnets.
* **Differentiator:** The ArgosBot acts as a "maestro" — it teaches the user to live safely in a connected world. When the user scans a new IoT device (e.g. a camera), the bot analyses known vulnerabilities and teaches good practices. Notifications explain what happened and how the system protected the home, without causing paranoia.

**One-line narrative:** "The smart home is not a distant future; it is the fridge, the Alexa and the washing machine you have today. The danger lies in simple devices the user does not suspect. ARGOS raises awareness of a present need, protecting and educating before the scale of the problem becomes catastrophic."

---

===== 3. Experience Delivered to the User =====

* **Awareness:** The user discovers which devices are at risk and why, in plain language.
* **Control:** Dashboard of protected devices, commands, logs, and alerts from a single app.
* **Learning:** ArgosBot answers questions, analyses devices (e.g. by photo), and suggests safe habits.
* **Engagement:** Gamification (e.g. White Hat Wizard mini-game) to build habit without losing the educational focus.
* **Trust:** Notifications that explain incidents and how ARGOS reacted, reinforcing that the system is in control.

---

===== 4. Jobs To Be Done =====

* **Emotional job:** "Feel safe at home without having to become a security expert."
* **Functional job:** "Teach the user to live safely in a connected world."
* **Outcome:** The user adopts safer behaviour and understands why certain devices and habits matter, while ARGOS handles detection and response in the background.

---

===== 5. Target Audiences (Canvas) =====

==== B2C — Primary =====
* **Segment:** Middle class, 25–50 years old, non-technical, often reactive to security issues.
* **Persona (extreme):** "The connected parent" — cares about home security, has IoT devices, but lacks time or knowledge to configure complex firewalls.
* **Validated insight:** This segment consumes low-cost technology without considering factory security; they feel safe until presented with vulnerability data (e.g. fridges, cheap voice assistants), then perception shifts.

==== B2B — Secondary =====
* **Segment:** Banks, fintechs, institutions with critical infrastructure.
* **Problem:** They lose large sums to botnet attacks fuelled by vulnerable home devices.
* **Solution:** ARGOS addresses the root cause (homes). Partnerships and B2B deals fund or subsidise protection for the middle class; corporates invest in reducing the botnet army that targets them.

---

===== 6. Business Model Summary =====

* **Freemium (B2C):** 2–3 devices protected free; full access to the educational ArgosBot; mini-game included. Goal: awareness and brand authority.
* **Premium (e.g. R$ 10/month):** Unlimited devices; advanced settings; proactive monitoring and ArgosBot support.
* **B2B:** Partnerships with financial institutions; white-label or subsidised access for their customers; selling the solution to the root problem, not just the product.

---

===== 7. Proof-of-Concept and Demo Strategy =====

* **Live hacking:** Visual demonstration using the **ARGOS Car** and **ARGOS Alexa**.
* **Storyline:** Attacker enters the network through a vulnerable "Alexa China" device; ARGOS detects and blocks escalation to the rest of the home (and to the car, when integrated). The app and/or physical feedback (e.g. LCD, brake) show "Hacked" vs "Attack blocked".
* **Goal:** Make the invisible risk visible and memorable; validate that ARGOS can detect and respond in a controlled environment.

---

===== 8. Current Implementation Status =====

==== Application (argos-app) =====
* Auth (JWT, 2FA, TOTP, register, login, refresh) and IoT endpoints (devices, command, logs, alerts, Bluetooth) in progress.
* Frontend: AuthContext, login, 2FA, verify-email, totp-setup; home, devices, logs, advanced-logs, argos-bot, new-device in progress.
* Chatbot: Argos assistant, intents, optional LLM, /ai/* endpoints in progress.
* Gamification (White Hat Wizard): Not yet started.

==== Car (argos-car) =====
* Stage 1: Raspberry Pi Zero W, L298N, JGA25 12V differential drive, HC-SR04. Code in argos-architecture/src. Checkpoints documented in Zim.
* Steering by differential drive (no servo). Single RPi for now; dual-brain (RPi + ESP32) is a documented evolution for later.

==== Alexa China (argos_alexa) =====
* Stage 2: Documentation and planning. ESP32 as vulnerable voice device; hardware refs: INMP441, PAM8403, speaker 4Ω 3W. Goals: wake word ("Hey ARGOS"), basic commands, attack simulation, IDS and system response. Communication ESP32 ↔ car (RPi) planned via UART.

===== 9. New Ideas and Roadmap (Mecatronics & Product) =====

==== From mecatronics and Zim (argos-car, argos_alexa) =====
* **Dual-brain evolution (documented, not yet implemented on car):** RPi as "central nervous system" (camera, AI, navigation, storage); ESP32 as "peripheral" (sensors, servo, LCD, audio, CAN). UART between them. Optional for a future phase.
* **Personality system (vision):** ARGOS as a character with emotions — LCD messages, R2-D2 style beeps, servo "antenna" for expression. State machine: command valid → happy (LCD "YES SIR!", beeps, antenna up); invalid → confused.
* **Alexa China pivot:** From "complex dual-brain car" to "vulnerable Alexa" as the demo centre. Simpler narrative, stronger security message; ESP32 as both voice interface and attack vector.
* **Car ↔ app:** When the car is registered as a device, the backend sends commands (e.g. /command); app does not assume a custom client on the car unless documented in Checkpoint.
